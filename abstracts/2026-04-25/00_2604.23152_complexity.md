# Motifs Enrichment as a Driver of an Emergent Preferential Attachment in rewired random regular graphs

- **arXiv**: [2604.23152](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23152)
- **Date**: 2026-04-25
- **Category**: complexity
- **Authors**: Pawat Akara-pipattana, Sergei Nechaev
- **NOFX Relevance Score**: 0

## Abstract

We study the statistics of rewired random regular graphs within the canonical ensemble, in which the average number of triangles is controlled by the fugacity $λ$. Using mean-field arguments, we estimate the location of the transition point $λ^{-}$. We focus primarily on the triangle-enriched phase above $λ^{-}$, where the graph becomes a loosely connected web of clusters. This inter-cluster network is scale-free and exhibits a power-law vertex degree distribution $P(d) \sim d^{-γ}$, with $γ\approx 2$ independent of degree and size of the graph. We attribute this behavior to an "emergent preferential attachment" for triangle motifs, derive the exponent $γ$ with a mean-field approach, and speculate on a possible connection between the typical topology of inter-cluster triangles and Efimov states in a conformally invariant potential.
